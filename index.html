Who We Are

General Internal Medicine at the University of Manitoba is an approximately 50 person section split between three acute care hospitals. We staff the Clinical Teaching Units (at all sites), see patients in clinic, provide education to residents and students, and are involved in administrative duties. We meet monthly for journal clubs or section rounds in a casual setting as well. We work as a team in providing patient-centred care.

Our Vision

Excellence in Clinical Care

To become researchers in various niche areas.

To contribute as educators to our academic community.

Expectations of New Recruits

  1. One year of training in area of interest, preferably abroad (can be R5 year)
  2. Research project developed during fellowship
  3. An ability to work on a team
  4. Self-drive and motivation to grow a specific area of interest through cultivating relationships with others and networking.
  5. Flexibility and reliability
